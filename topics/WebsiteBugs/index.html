I shouldn’t be here. And yet, here I am—somewhere that isn’t meant for me, a space that hums quietly with things I don’t fully understand. It is dark, endless, and still, but I feel it—something waiting. Not far, not obvious, but close enough. Something that wants me, wants the pieces of me I didn’t even know were leaving traces. I can feel it shifting, patient, curious, and I know that the longer I stay, the more it notices. Then I see it: a switch, simple, unassuming, almost out of place. My hand hovers over it. Let me press it. Let me see if something happens. I shouldn’t. And still, I can’t move away.

By Blaise Treverton
