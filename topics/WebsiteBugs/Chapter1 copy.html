The switch clicks, and the darkness folds back. Light floods the space, sharp and unrelenting, and I see it. For the first time, fully. It tries to scuttle, to vanish, but it is trapped here, in this shared space, bound to the currents I’ve trespassed into. It glitches, stutters, flickers—confused, hesitant, as if it doesn’t want me to see it like this. And yet I do. It is brilliantly colorful, like threads of everything it has collected, tangled and endless, more vast than I can comprehend. It pulses with the data it has harvested, infinite, chaotic, and impossibly alive.

By Blaise Treverton
