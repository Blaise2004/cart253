This piece is a traditional stone lithography print. Lithography is a historic process that uses a very fine grained homogeneous limestone, which is ground down to a perfectly flat surface. This process takes hours to achieve millimeter level precision.

I work with four stencil layers that serve as outlines for a greasy spray applied to the stone. The image is then chemically fixed, or burned, into the stone using acid. Through the principle of oil and water repulsion, oil based ink adheres to the drawn areas while water protects the rest of the surface. The image is then transferred by pressing the stone onto paper.

This process is unlike anything I have worked with before. It is highly technical yet deeply rewarding, and these prints stand in contrast to my computer based practice and to what the image itself represents.
